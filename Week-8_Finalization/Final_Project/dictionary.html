What is a Dictionary Attack?

A Dictionary Attack is when attackers use a pre-defined list of common passwords to gain access to accounts. It's faster than random guessing because many people reuse simple passwords.

This simulator will help you understand the risks and test your password strength.

Tip 1

Avoid common passwords.

Tip 2

Use complex combinations.

Tip 3

Long passwords resist attacks.
